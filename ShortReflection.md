# Short Reflection

## What Felt Confusing?

Initially, the idea of "Layer-2" was confusing because it sounded technical, like infrastructure or any terminology. I kept trying to understand it as backend logic or governance implementation.

The challenge was realizing that Layer-2 is not about execution or intelligence. It's about visibility and control.

Another confusing aspect was resisting the urge to overbuild. It's natural to want to add real logic, but the assignment intentionally restricts that.

## What Felt Powerful?

What felt powerful was realizing that governance can be expressed purely through UI.

Even without real enforcement logic:

- A refusal state changes behavior.
- Status indicators influence decisions.
- Visual sequencing creates structure without execution.

It became clear that design alone can shape trust, understanding and perceived control.


## What Must Never Be Allowed in Layer-2?

Layer-2 must never:

- Pretend to execute intelligence.
- Hide constraints.
- Blur the boundary between simulation and real logic.

Layer-2 should never become deceptive.

Its purpose is clarity, explicit boundaries and trust.

If Layer-2 becomes opaque, it defeats its own purpose.
